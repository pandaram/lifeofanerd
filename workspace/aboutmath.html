This page is fueled by pi . . .

Math. There's a reason why you're forced to memorize a bunch of theorems and formulas at school. Is that reason that you're going to use them in your adult life? Honestly, no, probably not. You won't need to know the quadratic formula or the unit circle. (Unless you become an engineer or mathematician, but, chances are, you probably won't. I mean, it would be cool if you did though) But the truth is, math is all around you. Math governs our world, and so much more. You wouldn't be able to read this on what ever interface you're using, laptop or smartphone, if it weren't for math. And I know what you're thinking. Only a mathematician would say that. And that's true. But aren't we all mathematicians in a way? Just like mathematicians, we all use logic everyday (or at least I hope we all do . . .). We try to find the beauty and order in the mess of chaos. What's there not to like about math?

Also, all seriousness aside, we've got an awesome sense of humor.
In fact, in honor of math humor, I have dedicated a page of this website to math memes.
